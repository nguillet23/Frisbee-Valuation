A deep dive into the methodology, findings, and implications of the OEFF prediction model.

The Problem

Offensive efficiency (OEFF) is one of the most important team-level metrics in competitive Ultimate Frisbee — but it's a lagging indicator. The question: can we predict OEFF from individual player statistics before the season ends?

"If we can identify which player behaviors drive team OEFF, coaches can make targeted adjustments mid-season rather than reacting after the fact."

The Data

Player statistics from 2021 through 2025 across professional leagues. After applying a minimum games filter, 2,149 clean player-season records span across 16 candidate features including plus/minus, completion percentage, opportunities, blocks, and more.

Feature Selection

The best model subset was identified through forward selection, evaluating 23 features. Plus/minus differential (▼) is the dominant predictive signal — substantially outpacing volume stats at every evaluation threshold.

The top predictive features — plus/minus, OPP, and Cmp% — capture the core efficiency signal across all five seasons of data.

Model Selection

Multiple model types were evaluated via 5-fold cross-validation. Gradient Boosting led with the highest CV R² (0.4814), followed by several Random Forest configurations. Linear models were also tested as baselines.

Residual Analysis

Residuals are distributed near zero across the dataset. Outliers tend to be players with extreme volume stats in a single season — pointing to the value of per-game normalization as a future improvement. Year-over-year consistency in residual patterns suggests stable model behavior.
